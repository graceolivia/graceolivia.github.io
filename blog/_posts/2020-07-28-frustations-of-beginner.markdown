One of the most frustrating things about being at my stage of programming is when my pride at a tiny sandcastle of achievement is obliterated by a tidal-wave reminder of how much I don't know.

I completed CS50 and was beginning to feel, dare I say it -? a little bit good about myself. I knew how to make a flask app, I could figure out how to add a javascript snippet here and there, and boy was I good at changing CSS colors!

Ha. Ha. Ha.

Anyway, launching into learning some different data structures. This stuff comes with a rather stiff learning curve. Also, I can barely figure out how to customize my Jekyll site, which is frustrating, because I know it's probably trivial. Just can't figure out where to change the code.

I hope I'll look back on this blog post soon, after I figured some things out, and smile at how daunted I was by simple things. Wish me luck!
